Coffee Market Analysis: Data-Driven Sales Strategy

A portfolio project analyzing 1,300+ coffee reviews to identify key market segments, understand the language of quality, and provide actionable recommendations to strengthen e-commerce sales.

Phase 1: Data Preparation & Exploration

The dataset was cleaned by dropping null values and removing duplicated/irrelevant columns. Text data was pre-processed using NLTK (tokenization, stop-word removal) to prepare for sentiment analysis.

Rating Distribution

The dataset exhibits a strong skew towards high ratings, indicating the source data is highly curated.

Phase 2: Market Segmentation via K-Means Clustering

K-Means was applied to the normalized features **Rating, Price (100g_USD), and Sentiment (Compound Score)**. The optimal number of clusters was determined to be four.

Segment Visualization

Cluster Characteristics

The four clusters reveal distinct market segments, from the high-volume, lower-price 'Daily Drinker' to the extremely high-value 'Luxury Collector'.

Phase 3: The Language of Quality (Keyword Analysis)

Positive keywords from the highest-rated coffees (95+ points) were extracted to understand consumer language.

Top Keywords in High-Rated Coffees (95+)

The highest-rated coffees are described using **technical, structure-focused language**, confirming the target consumer is sophisticated.

Phase 4: Actionable Sales Recommendations

By cross-referencing data insights with the marketing strategies of leading competitor websites (Kakalove Cafe, JBC Roasters, Paradise Roasters), we propose a 3-point strategy to strengthen sales:

1. Data-Driven Product Descriptions

Use the high-frequency terms: **'mouthfeel,' 'structure,' 'finish,'** and **'juicy'** directly in product copy. This validates the product's quality for the technical consumer.

2. Targeted Segmentation and Branding

  • **For High-End Beans:** Adopt **The Paradise Roasters Strategy** (luxury marketing, scarcity, 'Allocation Lists').
  • **For High-Rated, Mid-Price Beans:** Adopt **The JBC Strategy** (human story, sustainability, strong social proof).
  • **For All Beans:** Use the **Rating Score** prominently, as seen on the **Kakalove Cafe** website, to build immediate consumer trust.

3. Eliminate Defects

The low-rated keywords like **'scorched'** and **'fir'** indicate technical faults. Quality control must ensure these defects are eliminated, as they are a direct barrier to achieving high ratings and customer satisfaction.
