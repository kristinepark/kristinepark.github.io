Coffee Market Analysis: Data-Driven Sales Strategy

A portfolio project analyzing 1,300+ coffee reviews to identify key market segments, understand the language of quality, and provide actionable recommendations to strengthen e-commerce sales.

Phase 1: Data Preparation & Exploration

The dataset was cleaned by dropping null values and removing duplicated/irrelevant columns. Text data was pre-processed using NLTK (tokenization, stop-word removal) and VADER sentiment analysis to prepare the data for subsequent modeling.

Rating Distribution

The dataset exhibits a strong skew towards high ratings, indicating the source data is highly curated and focuses on specialty-grade coffees.

Phase 2: Process Automation & Data Collection

A full-lifecycle solution was designed to automate data collection and quality control, demonstrating an ability to design the data pipeline, not just analyze the output.

This system uses **Formstack** to create digital submission forms for roasters and internal Quality Control (QC) checks, with the data pipe-lined directly into **Smartsheet** as the central, collaborative database.

Crucially, **Smartsheet Flow Automation** was implemented to trigger immediate alerts to the QC team when a new entry registers a rating below 89 points or contains low-rated keywords like "scorched," ensuring rapid intervention and preventing low-quality products from reaching the market. This creates a powerful, automated feedback loop.

Phase 3: Market Segmentation via K-Means Clustering

K-Means was applied to the normalized features **Rating, Price (100g_USD), and Sentiment (Compound Score)**. The optimal number of clusters was determined to be four, revealing distinct customer segments.

Segment Visualization

Cluster Characteristics

The four clusters reveal distinct market segments, from the high-volume, lower-price 'Daily Drinker' to the extremely high-value 'Luxury Collector'. The strategy must be tailored to these groups.

Phase 4: The Language of Quality (Keyword Analysis)

Positive keywords from the highest-rated coffees (95+ points) were extracted to understand consumer language and identify selling points.

Top Keywords in High-Rated Coffees (95+)

The highest-rated coffees are described using **technical, structure-focused language**, confirming the target consumer is sophisticated and values complex attributes beyond simple flavor notes.

Phase 5: Strategic Recommendations

By cross-referencing data insights with the marketing strategies of leading competitor websites (Kakalove Cafe, JBC Roasters, Paradise Roasters), we propose a 3-point strategy to strengthen sales:

1. Data-Driven Product Descriptions

Use the high-frequency terms: **'mouthfeel,' 'structure,' 'finish,'** and **'juicy'** directly in product copy. This validates the product's quality for the technical consumer.

2. Targeted Segmentation and Branding

  • **For Luxury Collectors (Highest Cluster):** Adopt **The Paradise Roasters Strategy** (luxury marketing, scarcity, 'Allocation Lists').
  • **For Quality Enthusiasts (Mid-High Cluster):** Adopt **The JBC Strategy** (human story, sustainability, strong social proof).
  • **For All Coffees:** Use the **Coffee Review Rating Score** prominently, as seen on the **Kakalove Cafe** website, to build immediate consumer trust.

3. Automate Defect Prevention

The low-rated keywords like **'scorched'** and **'fir'** indicate technical faults. The automated Smartsheet flow (Phase 2) is critical for ensuring these defects are identified and eliminated at the QC level before they reach the consumer, protecting brand integrity.
